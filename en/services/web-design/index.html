Services

Websites that get found and get results

I design and build websites for small and medium-sized businesses – from concept to launch and maintenance. The most important choice isn't the look but the technology: it determines how easy the site is to update and what maintenance costs over the years. That's why we go through the options together before a single line of code.

Approaches – which one fits you?

WordPress

When you update content yourself

The world's most used publishing platform. Content, images and news are updated in the browser without technical skills, and plugins add features like e-commerce (WooCommerce), booking calendars or multilingual support.

Best when: content changes often, several people update the site, or you need a shop or blog.

Good to know: WordPress needs ongoing care – security updates and web hosting. I offer maintenance as a service.

Static site

When speed and zero worry matter most

A hand-coded HTML+CSS site with no publishing system, hosted on a modern cloud platform (GitHub + Netlify). No database, no plugins – nothing to hack or patch every month.

Best when: content rarely changes and you want the best speed and search visibility with minimal running costs.

Good to know: content changes go through me – small updates are handled quickly and affordably, and hosting is typically free.

Static + CMS

Speed and self-editing combined

A modern middle ground (e.g. Sanity): the site is as fast and secure as a static one, but content is edited in the browser through an easy admin panel – without the WordPress maintenance burden.

Best when: you want to update texts and images yourself but don't need WordPress's plugin jungle.

Good to know: it takes more work to build than a plain static site, so it pays off when self-editing is a real need.
